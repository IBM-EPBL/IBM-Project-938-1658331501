Briefing

  • Earthquake

    a sudden violent shaking of the ground, typically causing great destruction, as a result of movements within the earth's crust or volcanic action

  • Cyclone

    a system of winds rotating inwards to an area of low barometric pressure, with an anticlockwise (northern hemisphere) or clockwise (southern hemisphere) circulation; a depression.

  • Flood

    Floods can potentially increase the transmission of water- and vector-borne diseases. Find out more about risks associated with flooding on WHO's official website.

  • Wild Fire

    wildfire, also called wildland fire, uncontrolled fire in a forest, grassland, brushland, or land sown to crops. The terms forest fire, brush fire, etc., may be used to describe specific types of wildfires; their usage varies according to the characteristics of the fire and the region in which it occurs.
